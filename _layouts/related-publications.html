HBCUs propel African American male mathematics majors.

Published by: Jett, C. C. (2013).

While researchers have examined the educational experiences of African American male students at historically black colleges and universities (HBCUs), researchers have neglected to adequately hone in on the mathematics experiences of African American male students at these institutions. Read more here.

You Would Not Believe What I Have to Go Through to Prove My Intellectual Value!

Published by: McGee, E. O., & Martin, D. B. (2011b).

Stereotype management is introduced to explain high achievement and resilience among 23 Black mathematics and engineering college students. Read more here.

Assume the Position . . . You Fit the Description.

Published by: Smith, W. A., Allen, W. R., & Danley, L. L. (2007).

The present study examines the experiences of 36 Black male students, in focus group interviews, enrolled at Harvard University; Michigan State University; University of California, Berkeley; University of Illinois; and the University of Michigan. Two themes emerged: (a) anti-Black male stereotyping and marginality (or Black misandry), which caused (b) extreme hypersurveillance and control. Read more here.
